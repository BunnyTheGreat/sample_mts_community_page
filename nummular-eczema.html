Nummular Eczema

Nummular eczema is a form of eczema that causes round or oval-shaped patches of irritated, itchy skin. The name "nummular" comes from the Latin word for "coin," referring to the distinctive shape of the lesions. These patches most often appear on the arms, legs, hands, or torso and can become dry, scaly, or even ooze during more severe flares. Nummular eczema is often associated with very dry skin and may develop after a skin injury, insect bite, or burn.

Understanding Nummular Eczema: The 5 W's

Who Gets It? Can occur at any age but most commonly in adults between 50-80 years. May appear more frequently in men. Often associated with dry skin and previous history of other eczema types.
What Causes It? Causes aren't fully understood, but triggers include dry skin, skin irritants, insect bites, allergic contact dermatitis, and emotional stress. More common in winter months when humidity is low.
Where Does It Appear? Usually appears on the lower legs, hands, feet, and torso. Lesions are typically well-defined, round or coin-shaped, ranging from 1-3 cm in diameter. Can appear on arms, buttocks, and face.
When Does It Occur? Often develops suddenly without preceding rash or inflammation. More common in cold, dry seasons. Each outbreak typically lasts several weeks to months. Can recur over years.
How Long Does It Last? Individual patches can persist for months if untreated. With proper care, lesions typically improve in 4-8 weeks. The condition may become chronic with recurrent flares throughout the year or remain dormant for extended periods.
